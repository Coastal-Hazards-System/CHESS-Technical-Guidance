# CEM Modernization Plan

_Modernization assessment of the USACE Coastal Engineering Manual (EM 1110-2-1100), with a crosswalk of CEM content to key coastal-engineering topic areas. Generated 2026-06-24._

## Status & method

The CEM chapters were drafted ~1998–2008 and last materially updated by **Change 3 (2011)**. Each Part was assessed by a subject-matter agent (with web research) for content that is outdated relative to current (2020s) practice, standards, datasets, models, and guidance.

> **Verification:** the items below were independently fact-checked by an AI verification pass (2026-06-25) against authoritative current sources: USACE Publications and ERDC-CHL (Coastal Hazards System, Engineering With Nature), NOAA, USGS, FEMA, CIRIA, the EurOtop manual, and peer-reviewed literature. Every high-priority item was confirmed, meaning the cited CEM content is genuinely dated and the named modern replacement is the current standard, successor tool, or governing USACE policy. Corrections surfaced by that pass have been folded in, notably **EP 1100-2-1 (2019)** as the current implementing procedure in place of the superseded ETL 1100-2-1, and **ASCE 7-22 Chapter 5** for coastal flood loads (Chapter 6 covers tsunami). The 12-topic crosswalk (final section) is compiled from the CEM text directly.
